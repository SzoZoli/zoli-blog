**Expert explanation:**
The recent work by Miranda Schwacke, a PhD student at MIT, represents a significant advancement in the development of sustainable artificial intelligence through the use of electrochemical devices inspired by brain function. This research is pivotal as it seeks to create computing systems that not only emulate human-like intelligence but do so in a more energy-efficient and environmentally friendly manner.

The main technology driving this trend is brain-inspired computing, often referred to as neuromorphic computing. This approach contrasts with traditional silicon-based hardware by incorporating materials and mechanisms that mirror the electrochemical processes of biological neurons. This innovation has the potential to drastically reduce the energy consumption of AI systems, addressing one of the critical concerns regarding the environmental impact of data centers and AI model training.

The sectors most affected by this breakthrough are technology and energy, particularly industries focused on AI development, data processing, and large-scale machine learning applications. By enhancing the sustainability of AI systems, Schwacke's research could lead to more responsible and efficient use of AI across various domains, including healthcare, transportation, and renewable energy, thus fostering a future where advanced technology aligns with ecological responsibility.
